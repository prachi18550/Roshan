APPS:

1. Skillsoft (Percipio)

What It Is: A digital learning platform with a large content library.

Its best features –

  • Short learning videos, audiobooks, and practice labs.
  • Courses in leadership, communication, compliance, and technology.
  • Enterprise-level platform also used by companies.

Best For: Professionals wanting to improve soft skills or learn leadership.

Website: https://www.skillsoft.com

2. VirtualSpeech

What It Is: An innovative app combining VR and e-learning to teach communication.

Its best Features:

  • Practice public speaking in virtual environments (like interviews, meetings).
  • Courses on presentation, leadership, sales pitching.
  • Feedback on pacing, filler words, eye contact.

Best For: People preparing for interviews, presentations, or leadership roles.

Website: https://virtualspeech.com

3. edX

What It Is: A nonprofit MOOC (massive open online course) provider founded by MIT and Harvard.

Its best Features:

  • University-level courses in various disciplines.
  • Professional certificates and MicroMasters programs.
  • Free courses available; some certifications paid.

Best For: Lifelong learners, job changers, and professionals needing credentials.

Website: https://www.edx.org

ADDITIONAL RESOURCES

1. Coursera

What It Is: An online learning platform offering university-level courses and degrees.

Its best Features:

  • Courses from Yale, Stanford, Google, Meta, etc.
  • Career Certificates in IT Support, Data Analytics, UX Design.
  • Audit for free; certificates are paid.

Best For: People aiming for credentials from recognized institutions.

Website: https://www.coursera.org

2. Udemy

What It Is: A course marketplace with both free and paid content.

Its best Features:

  • Over 200,000 video courses in business, design, development, etc.
  • Frequent discounts; many courses under $20 or free.
  • Lifetime access to purchased content.

Best For: Affordable, skill-based learning in a wide variety of areas.

Website: https://www.udemy.com

3. FutureLearn

What It Is: A UK-based online education provider.

Its best Features:

  • Free short courses, ExpertTracks, and Microcredentials.
  • Offered by universities and cultural institutions.
  • Topics in healthcare, business, teaching, psychology, etc.

Best For: Broad career-oriented courses and professional development.

Website: https://www.futurelearn.com

4. OpenLearn (by The Open University)

What It Is: A free learning platform by the UK’s Open University.

Its best Features:

  • Short and in-depth courses in psychology, health, money, tech.
  • Downloadable content and statements of participation.
  • Best For: General learners and those exploring new subject areas.

Website: https://www.open.edu/openlearn

Free Online Learning Platforms

1. freeCodeCamp

What It Is: A nonprofit organization offering a free, self-paced coding curriculum.

Its best Features:

  • Full coding curriculum (HTML, CSS, JavaScript, Python, etc.)
  • Real-world projects for nonprofits to build a portfolio.
  • Certifications in Responsive Web Design, JavaScript Algorithms, APIs, Machine Learning, and more.

Best For: Aspiring developers, data scientists, and those seeking tech careers.

Website: https://www.freecodecamp.org

2. Google Digital Garage

What It Is: A free learning platform by Google offering career and business development courses.

Its best Features:

  • Courses on data, tech, and digital marketing.
  • Industry-recognized certifications (e.g., Fundamentals of Digital Marketing).
  • Flexible, video-based lessons.

Best For: People wanting to grow digital marketing or data literacy skills.

Website: https://digitalgarage.withgoogle.com

3. IBM SkillsBuild

What It Is: A global skills training platform focused on job readiness in technology.

Its best Features:

  • Courses in cybersecurity, AI, cloud computing, workplace skills.
  • Personalized learning paths and digital credentials.
  • Built-in mentorship programs for career development.

Best For: People interested in entry-level tech careers or career changers.

Website: https://skillsbuild.org

4. Khan Academy

What It Is: A nonprofit offering free, high-quality education for all ages.

Its best Features:

  • K–12 subjects, SAT prep, financial literacy, computer science.
  • Progress tracking, practice exercises, and instructional videos.
  • Translated into multiple languages.

Best For: Students, adults refreshing academic knowledge, or job seekers upskilling.

Website: https://www.khanacademy.org

5. OpenClassrooms

What It Is: An online education platform with career-focused learning programs.

Its best Features:

  • Courses in software development, project management, UX design, etc.
  • Mentorship with industry professionals.
  • Project-based learning.

    Best For: Learners seeking a career path in tech and professional services.
